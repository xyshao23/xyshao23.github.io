About Me

I am a full-time Engineering Ph.D. student (2023 intake) at Tsinghua University (SIGS), jointly trained by Tsinghua and Huawei. I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Xiu Li at the Institute of Data and Information.

My research lies at the intersection of Embodied AI and Robotics, with a primary focus on Generative Models, Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models, and World Models. I am particularly driven by the challenge of moving beyond traditional end-to-end VLA models by exploring decoupled perception-control architectures and pushing the boundaries of imitation learning in low-data regimes. Currently, I am deeply engaged in building robust pipelines for long-horizon complex robotic manipulations, and rethinking embodied representations by leveraging Flow Matching and Velocity Fields as zero-shot kinematic priors.
